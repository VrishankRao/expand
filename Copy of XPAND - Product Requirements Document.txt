XPAND — Product Requirements Document
Version: v2.0 (supersedes v1.0) Status: Approved for build Build window: 8 weeks · 4 sprints · 3 interns Audience: Engineering team, stakeholders

One-line pitch: XPAND is the front door for the WhatsApp-first economy — your WhatsApp Profile in one link, with a built-in lead inbox that turns visitor intent into real conversations.

1. Why this exists
WhatsApp has stopped being a messaging app. In India and similar markets, it is the primary business interface — the place where coaches teach, communities gather, creators sell, and operators run their day-to-day.
The tooling around it has not caught up. A coach running a free community, a paid mastermind, a broadcast channel, and a support chat has no clean way to present them as a unified presence. Worse: even when they share a wa.me link, they get clicks without context. They don't know who clicked, what they wanted, or whether the visitor ever joined.
XPAND solves both problems:
The presence problem — one link at xpand.so/<handle> that unifies every WhatsApp surface the user runs.
The intent problem — every visitor who wants to engage fills a lead form first. The user gets a name, an email, a WhatsApp number, and a message — not a click count.
We are not building a link page. We are building a category: the WhatsApp Profile.

2. The product noun
The page a user creates on XPAND is called their WhatsApp Profile.
Not "hub." Not "link page." Not "bio link."
It is their public WhatsApp presence — what someone sees about them before they have their number.
This noun appears in:
Public page header: "<Name>'s WhatsApp Profile"
Dashboard title: "Your WhatsApp Profile"
Onboarding flow: "Let's build your WhatsApp Profile"
OG share preview: "<Name>'s WhatsApp Profile · Powered by XPAND"
Marketing tagline candidate: "Your WhatsApp Profile, in one link"
If a Kiro task generates copy that calls it anything else, fix it before merging.

3. The strategic shift in v2
The original spec ended at clicks. We were going to count taps on links and call that "analytics." Two problems:
Clicks are vanity. A coach knowing 247 people tapped her group link doesn't tell her who they were, what they wanted, or if they ever joined.
Clicks don't compound. Every click is forgotten the moment the visitor lands in WhatsApp. The user gets nothing back.
v2 fixes both. Every CTA on every public profile routes through a lead form. The visitor enters name, email, WhatsApp number, and message. Only then do they get the WhatsApp link. The user sees that lead in their Inbox — labelled by source, searchable, exportable.
This single change does four things:
Eliminates vanity metrics. Every lead is a real person with real intent.
Creates the data layer that becomes the moat. XPAND now holds the user's audience graph, not just their click counts.
Makes the Pro tier obvious. Free = N leads/month. Pro = unlimited + WhatsApp BSP routing in Phase 2.
Justifies "WhatsApp Profile" as the noun. A profile that captures leads IS a profile. A page that redirects to WhatsApp is just a page.

5. End-to-end user journey (the happy path)
Discovery — Priya sees a coach in her network using xpand.so/<handle>. She visits the page on her phone, sees a clean WhatsApp Profile with four labelled surfaces and CTAs like "Join community" and "Send DM."
Signup — She taps the XPAND Love Sign in the footer. Lands on signup. Enters her WhatsApp number. Gets a 6-digit OTP via SMS. Verifies.
Handle claim — First screen: "Claim your handle." Types priyacoaches. Available. Continues.
First link — Second screen: "Paste your first WhatsApp link." She pastes her free community invite. Auto-detection says "Group." She labels it "Free Community" and the CTA defaults to "Join community."
Profile goes live — Her profile is now live at xpand.so/priyacoaches with one link card.
Add more — She adds her channel, paid mastermind, support chat. Each link auto-gets a CTA: "Follow channel," "Join community," "Send DM."
Add email — Dashboard prompts her: "Add your email to get notified when someone messages you." She adds it, verifies via OTP, done.
Share — Dashboard prominently shows her profile URL with a copy button. She pastes it into her Instagram bio.
First lead — Within minutes, someone fills out her "Join community" form. Priya gets an email: "You have a new lead from Free Community." She taps through to the Inbox.
The wow moment — She sees the lead: "Anjali Mehta · anjali@example.com · +91 98xxxxxxxx · 'Hi Priya, saw your post on Instagram, would love to join.'" This is not a click. This is a real person.
Return — day 2 — She comes back to check the Inbox. Five new leads. Three from Free Community, two from Send DM. She didn't know Send DM would convert at all.
Hooked — She returns daily because the Inbox tells her what's working AND brings her real people, not numbers.
This is the journey every PRD ticket should serve. If a ticket doesn't move this journey forward, defer it.

7. Success metrics
North Star
5,000 active users. Active = a user with at least one link on their profile who has received at least one lead in the last 30 days.
This number is the trigger to:
Invest in XPAND as a real venture under Foundership
Begin v2 work on the workflow layer (WhatsApp BSP integration, broadcasting)
Roll out paid pricing
Leading indicators (track weekly)
% of users with 3+ links + CTAs on their profile
% of users who verify their email (predicts engagement)
% of profiles receiving ≥1 lead within 7 days of going live
% of users opening the Inbox in week 2 or later
Median leads per user per week
Form completion rate (form started → form submitted)
% of users who share their XPAND link publicly within 24 hours
Anti-metrics — do not optimize for these
Raw signups (easy to vanity-game)
Page views (replaced by leads as the real metric)
Clicks (we're now measuring leads, which is downstream of clicks anyway)
Time-on-dashboard (Inbox should be quick to scan)

8. Positioning rules
These hold across all UI copy, marketing copy, and team communication.
We are not a link-in-bio tool. Never frame XPAND in Linktree's category.
We are not a contact form widget. We are a WhatsApp Profile that happens to capture leads.
We are not a WhatsApp Business API reseller. Phase 2 may integrate one, v1 does not need one.
We are not a CRM. The Inbox is read-only in v1; CRM features (tagging, segmentation, broadcasting) are v2.
We are not an event platform. No ticketing, RSVPs, or event tooling — ever, in this product.
We are WhatsApp-first, not WhatsApp-only. Users can add non-WhatsApp links classified as "Other," but they cannot have CTAs — only direct redirects.

9. Brand & voice
Palette: Deep Indigo (#1E1B4B) + Emerald Green (#22C55E). Green is the WhatsApp-native cue. Never drift to generic SaaS blue.
Voice: Punchy. Short paragraphs. Verb-driven. Founder-honest.
Words we use: clean, fast, real, ship, build, share, lead, message.
Words we avoid: enabling, empowering, leveraging, seamless, robust, journey, ecosystem.
Sentence length: under 15 words by default. If a sentence runs over, split it.
Punctuation: em dashes are fine. Exclamation marks are not, except in success states. No ellipses.
Worked examples:
❌ "We enable creators to seamlessly manage their WhatsApp presence."
✅ "Your WhatsApp Profile, in one link."
❌ "Unlock powerful insights about your audience"
✅ "Real leads. Not clicks."
❌ "Your engagement journey starts here..."
✅ "Get your first lead in minutes."

12. Glossary
Term
Meaning
WhatsApp Profile
The user's public page at xpand.so/<handle>. The product noun.
Surface
One of the WhatsApp link types: group, community, channel, chat, business, other.
CTA
The button copy on a link card. "Join group", "Send DM", etc.
Handle
The unique username at xpand.so/<handle>. URL-safe, 3–30 chars, lowercase, [a-z0-9_-].

14. 4-sprint build plan
Three sprints would be tight. Four sprints is comfortable for 3 interns.
Sprint 1 — Foundations (Weeks 1–2)
Goal: a logged-in user can claim a handle and see an empty profile shell (private).
Features touched: 01 (auth-onboarding), 02 (handle-reservation), 03 (profile-management shell), 11 (design-system foundations), 12 (observability bootstrap).
Demo: sign up with a real Indian phone number → receive OTP → verify → claim handle → see empty dashboard.
Sprint 2 — Profile core (Weeks 3–4)
Goal: a user can build a working profile and a visitor can see it (but not yet submit leads).
Features touched: 04 (link-parsing), 05 (link-crud with CTA assignment), 06 (public-profile-page).
Demo: paste 5 mixed WhatsApp links, see auto-classification with default CTAs, publish, share to a real WhatsApp group, see the profile render on visitor phones.
Sprint 3 — Leads (Weeks 5–6)
Goal: a visitor can submit a lead form and the user sees it in their Inbox.
Features touched: 07 (lead-capture), 08 (inbox), 09 (deep-link-redirect-ingest — repurposed for form events).
Demo: visitor taps a CTA, fills the form, submits, gets the WhatsApp link. User receives an email and sees the lead in their Inbox.
Sprint 4 — Analytics + polish (Weeks 7–8)
Goal: a user can see what's converting in real time. System is ready for public launch.
Features touched: 10 (analytics-dashboard), 11 (design-system polish), 12 (observability hardening).
Demo: soft-launch to XPAND Club + Foundership lists. First 50 real users, real leads in real Inboxes.
Detailed sprint plan with ticket allocation lives in /docs/sprint-plan.md.
